# How I Passed CISSP: A Strategic Approach

The Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP) exam is notorious for being "a mile wide and an inch deep." While many approach it as a purely technical exam, the reality is that it's a management exam. To pass, you must stop thinking like a hands-on engineer and start thinking like a Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) or a senior risk advisor.

## 1. Adopt the "Manager Mindset"
When faced with a scenario on the exam, the knee-jerk reaction for an engineer is to fix the technical problem immediately (e.g., "reboot the server" or "block the IP"). 

However, the CISSP wants you to follow the process. The correct answer is often:
- Assess the risk.
- Consult the policy.
- Report to senior management.
- Protect human life first.

## 2. Master the Domains, Don't Just Memorize
I found that brute-force memorization of block ciphers or specific OSI layer protocols wasn't as helpful as understanding *why* they are used. You need to know the difference between symmetric and asymmetric encryption not just mathematically, but in terms of scalable key management and non-repudiation.

## 3. Study Materials that Actually Worked
- **The Official Study Guide (OSG)**: Essential for the foundational knowledge, though quite dry.
- **Practice Exams (Boson & Official)**: The key here wasn't getting the right answer, but understanding *why* the other three options were wrong.
- **Kelly Handerhan’s "Why You Will Pass the CISSP"**: This video is absolute gold for calibrating your mindset before walking into the testing center.

## 4. The Exam Experience
The Computerized Adaptive Testing (CAT) format means the exam gets harder the better you do. If you feel like you are failing because the questions are getting impossibly obscure, that's actually a good sign! Keep your composure.

Passing the CISSP validated my experience in bridging the gap between deep technical implementation and high-level enterprise risk. It changes how you view security—not as an absolute, but as a carefully calculated balancing act of risk and reward.
